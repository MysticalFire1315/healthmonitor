Help

What is HealthMonitor

HealthMonitor is an application designed to assist you in staying healthy. By connecting to Strava to get physical activity data, HealthMonitor can calculate if you met the government’s recommended weekly activity goals and identify a schedule personalised just for you based on your previous activities.


Getting Started / Tutorial

If you are new to this app, register an account by clicking on the Signup button in the top right corner of the screen to utilise and view the full functionality of this app. Fill up the form appropriately before clicking submit to create an account. For help on creating an account, refer to Help => Signup.

After logging in, you should be directed to the Dashboard. The Dashboard is where you can view your recorded stats after authorizing Strava. If this is your first time logging in, click on your username in the top right hand corner to reveal a dropdown menu. Within this menu, you can find the About tab which brings you to a page detailing the background of this app, a Help tab for help regarding the usage of this app, a settings button to view the information currently stored in your account, and a logout button to log out of your account.

Click on the settings button to access the Settings page. To the right of the words ‘Strava Authorisation (Help)’, you will notice that it says “Authorise Now”. This means that this app is not yet authorised to connect to Strava, and thus the app’s available functionality for you to use is extremely limited. Click on the “Authorise Now” hyperlink to be redirected to Strava’s authorisation page. Log in or sign up to Strava and grant access to this app to continue. Refer to Help => Strava Authorisation for more information regarding the app’s access to your Strava account.

In the Dashboard, you should now find that your total hours of activity in the last 7 days is displayed, along with whether your weekly goal is met and your typical schedule (if any). This weekly goal is set depending on the age group that you entered in the registration process and can be viewed from the Settings page but cannot be changed. Learn more about the weekly goal at Help => Weekly Goal. The typical schedule will not necessarily show up, as that depends on the pattern of your activities. If there are any patterns, they will be identified and will show up in your Dashboard.

Finally, you may have noticed that there is a tab named Strava Activities in the top left. Clicking on this will bring you to a page where you can view all the activities you have logged to Strava within the past 90 days4 (see Fig 4). The activities in the table will refresh after an hour if you leave the page, or you can manually refresh it by clicking on the words “Refresh now” under the table.

Click on the app name in the top left to return to the Dashboard. This is where the tutorial ends. Enjoy using the app!


Signup

There are 6 fields to fill out in the account registration form:

  • The 1st field is the email field, in which you should enter your email address. This is what will be used to identify you in the database and there cannot be two accounts with the same email, so ensure that your email address is unique.
  • The 2nd field is where you should enter your desired username. This username can be anything you like, so get creative!
  • The 3rd field is for your password. Make sure you remember your password, as there is no way to recover it!
  • You must type your password into the 4th field again. Ensure that it is the same as what you entered in the 3rd field.
  • Select your age group. Please be truthful, as the recommended weekly hours are based on the age group you select and there is no way to change this later.
  • Click on the check box, and follow the prompts if necessary. This is just to ensure that you’re not a robot. Beep!


Login

There are 2 fields to fill out in the login form:

  • Enter your email address in the 1st field.
  • Enter the password you registered with in the 2nd field.


Strava Authorisation

Access to Strava is an integral part of this application. Without access to Strava, most features of this application cannot be accessed.

To authorise access to Strava, head over to the settings page and click the “Authorise Now” button.

When prompted, log in to Strava and click “Authorize” when prompted.


Weekly Goal

The weekly physical activity goals are taken directly from the Australian government’s department of health website. It is said that for children between 5 and 17 years, the recommended number of hours of exercise per week is 3; whereas it’s 5 for adults between 18 to 64 years and 3.5 for seniors older than 64 years old.
